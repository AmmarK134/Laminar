The gap after discharge

You go home with a newborn — and almost no monitoring.

Serious postpartum complications often develop quietly at home. New mothers are overwhelmed, recovering physically, and sleep-deprived. Few people can tell, in the moment, which symptoms are normal and which need urgent attention.

"Is this normal?"

A headache, swelling, or blurred vision can be ordinary recovery — or an early warning of postpartum hypertension. It's hard to know which, alone at 3am.

Tracking falls apart

Blood-pressure checks at home are easy to forget when you're exhausted and caring for a newborn. Inconsistent readings hide the trend that actually matters.

The follow-up is too late

The standard postpartum visit can be weeks away. By then a dangerous pattern may already have escalated — or been missed entirely.

How it works

From a daily check-in to a clear next step.

1

Daily check-in

Each day Laminar sends a short, friendly prompt — no app fatigue, no clinical jargon.

2

Enter readings

The patient logs blood pressure and taps how she's feeling. It takes under a minute.

3

Patterns detected

Laminar reads each entry against postpartum thresholds and trends — not just single numbers.

4

Care team reviews

Concerning responses are flagged on a dashboard so clinicians see who needs attention first.

5

Clear guidance

The patient gets a plain-language next step: keep monitoring, or contact your care team now.

About Laminar

A startup built around the postpartum gap.

Laminar is a clinically informed health-tech startup focused on one thing: the fragile window between hospital discharge and full postpartum recovery.

We're motivated by how often serious complications develop quietly at home — and by how much earlier they could be caught with simple, consistent monitoring. We design around patient-centered communication and the real workflows care teams already use, so monitoring fits into recovery instead of adding to it.
